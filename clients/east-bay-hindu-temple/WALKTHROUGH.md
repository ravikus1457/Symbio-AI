## A walk through the new site

The site is **one smooth page** that visitors scroll through (with a menu to jump to any part):

1. **Welcome banner** — the temple's name in English and Hindi (पूर्वी खाड़ी हिन्दू मंदिर), a Sanskrit blessing (*Om Sarve Bhavantu Sukhinah*), a glowing lamp, and two clear buttons: *Plan Your Visit* and *Aarti & Darshan Timings*.
2. **Welcome message** — who the temple is and who it serves: a non-profit home for Hindu families across the East Bay, open to all.
3. **Our Deities (Darshan)** — six gold-arched niches: **Shri Ram Darbar, Shiv Parivar, Durga Maa, Shri Hanuman ji, Shri Ganesh ji, and Radha Krishna** — each with its name in Hindi and a short line.
4. **Darshan & Aarti Timings** — the weekly hours and the daily aarti schedule, easy to read.
5. **Poojas & Sevas** — the ceremonies the Pandit performs, at the temple and at homes (archana, abhishekam, havan, griha pravesh, weddings & sanskaars, Satyanarayan katha, memorial rites), with a *Call to Book* button.
6. **Festivals & Celebrations** — the year's major festivals with dates (Shivaratri, Holi, Ram Navami, Hanuman Jayanti, Janmashtami, Ganesh Chaturthi, Navratri, Diwali).
7. **Our Priest** — a feature on Pandit Rakesh Bhargav Ji, with a warm welcome message and buttons to call or book a pooja.
8. **Support the Temple (Daan)** — a respectful donation section that opens a giving popup (more below).
9. **Visit Us** — the address, phone numbers, an *Open now* indicator, a live Google Map, and a *Get Directions* button.

## Features that take care of themselves

These update on their own — no one has to edit the website:

- 🟢 **"Open now / Closed" indicator** — reads the temple's hours and tells visitors if it's open right now, and when it opens next.
- 📅 **Today's hours are highlighted** automatically in the weekly schedule.
- 🪔 **Festival countdown** — the page always shows *"X days until [next festival]"* and marks the next celebration, on its own.
- 📱 **Works beautifully on phones** — most devotees will visit from their phone, and it's built for that first.
- 🕉️ **Hindi & Sanskrit throughout** — deity names, blessings, and section titles appear in Devanagari alongside English.

## About the deity artwork

Each niche currently shows a genuine **public-domain painting by Raja Ravi Varma** (the renowned 19th-century artist whose works are out of copyright) — so the deity section looks complete and beautiful today, with no copyright concerns.

Two easy options going forward, whenever the temple prefers:
- Keep the classic paintings, or
- Replace any of them with **the temple's own photographs of its murtis** — the most personal and accurate choice.

*(Behind each painting is a hand-drawn gold emblem — bow & arrow for Ram, trishul for Shiv, lotus for Durga, gada for Hanuman, the elephant form for Ganesh, flute & feather for Krishna — which appears automatically if an image is ever unavailable, so a niche is never empty.)*
